Chapter 3: Conditions

There are many, many circumstances under which a character may become somehow impaired, such as losing their sight or ability to move, or gaining an awful feeling in their body or mind that makes performing actions more difficult. Listed and described here are several such conditions and their effects on a creature. Several conditions refer to another condition in their description; in such cases, the effects stack with each other.

Blinded. A blinded creature is unable to see, and as a result, all attacks against them have advantage, all attacks by them have disadvantage, and they automatically fail all sight-based skill and ability checks.

Charmed. The charmed condition grants advantage to all social skill and ability checks when made by the charmer to the charmed; in addition, the charmed creature cannot willingly attack the charmer.

Deafened. A deafened creature cannot hear, and automatically fails any and all hearing-based skill and ability checks. It also has disadvantage on Perception checks.

Frightened. When a creature is frightened, it makes all of its skill and ability checks with disadvantage while it can see the source of its fear, which it cannot willingly move closer to.

Grappled. A grappled creature cannot move and cannot benefit to any bonuses to its movement. Attacks made against a grappled creature by the creature grappling it have advantage. The condition ends if something forcibly removes the victim from the grappler’s grasp or if the grappler lets them go.

Incapacitated. An incapacitated creature cannot move and cannot take any actions or reactions except speech.

Invisible. A creature that is invisible cannot be visually perceived unless by special exception and has advantage on sight-based Stealth checks. They can still be perceived by disturbances in their environment however, such as the noises they make or any tracks they leave behind. In addition, attacks that they make against creatures who cannot see them have advantage and attacks against them by creatures who cannot see them have disadvantage.

Paralyzed. A paralyzed creature is incapacitated and its Dodge score is reduced to 0.

Petrified. A petrified creature is completely frozen as it is turned to ice, stone, or a similar material. It cannot regain or lose Health or take any kind of action or reaction. It is immune to psychic damage, automatically passes all Willpower saves, its Fortitude score becomes 5, its Dodge score becomes 0, and its Armor score becomes 20; if it takes any final damage, it starts to break apart, the severity of which being up to the amount of final damage taken and the DM’s discretion.

Prone. A prone creature can only crawl unless it uses an action to stand up. All attacks made by a prone creature have disadvantage, except Firearm weapon attacks, which have advantage. Melee attacks against a prone creature have advantage, while ranged attacks have disadvantage.

Restrained. A restrained creature is also considered grappled, however it can no longer make attacks and attacks against it have advantage.

Sickened. A sickened creature feels illness take root somewhere in their body, crippling them from the inside. All of a sickened creature’s checks, attacks, and saves are made with disadvantage, and attacks against it have advantage.

Slowed. A slowed creature can only perform one action on its turn, cannot use reactions, has its Dodge score reduced by half, and performs its attacks with disadvantage.

Stunned. A stunned creature is considered incapacitated, however can only speak falteringly and in stutters. In addition, attacks against it have advantage, and it makes all saves with disadvantage.

Unconscious. An unconscious creature is incapacitated, cannot speak, and is completely unaware of its surroundings. It drops whatever it is holding in its hands and falls prone unless something physically prevents it from doing so, and its Dodge score is reduced to 0.
