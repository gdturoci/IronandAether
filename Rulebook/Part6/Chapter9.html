Chapter 9: Languages

Sentient Languages

Given here is a list of all the languages able to be spoken by Sentients without arcane aid. For each, both the native speakers and a description of the central phonetic sounds are given. It takes 6 cumulative months of study to learn another Sentient language, or you can learn one whenever your INT score increases to the next whole number.

Common is a special language: it is the only language that a creature must be taught, and will not gain intrinsic knowledge of as they come of age. It is unlikely to be found in less-developed settlements, villages, and tribes.
Speakers. Everyone, unless explicitly stated otherwise.
Sounds. A utilitarian language that is equally accessible to all races, there is an even mix of hard and soft consonants and soft and guttural vowels.
Designed for Sentients who can’t speak or hear, Common Sign Language (CSL) allows two or more Sentients to communicate using hand movements and gestures.
Speakers. No native speakers, but can be learned by any Sentients who have two hands each having 4 fingers and 1 apposable thumb.
Sounds. Technically none, but words and punctuation are communicated using hand gestures, and inflection can be inferred from body language. Both “speakers” must be able to see each other.
Speakers. Corvium.
Sounds. Words and syllables flow together, usually melodically.
Speakers. Darklings.
Sounds. A whispered mix of hard consonants and soft vowels that flow and crash together in even measure.
Speakers. Dragons, Drakon, Kobolds, and Titans.
Sounds. Low, guttural sounds with drawn-out vowels and thick, harsh consonants.
Speakers. Dwarves.
Sounds. Short, halting, sometimes vowel-less words with thick and accentuated consonants.
Speakers. Elves.
Sounds. Flowy, melodic syllables with many vowels and soft consonants.
Speakers. Felinae.
Sounds. A mix of growls, purrs, hisses, and yowls. Both vowels and consonants are harsh.
Speakers. Goblins.
Sounds. Hard, guttural consonants and vowels, more often sounding like grunts than anything else.
Speakers. Humans.
Sounds. Thick, hard consonants with simple, sometimes melodic vowels.
Speakers. Monstrosities.
Sounds. Deep, guttural, thick consonants, trilled rhotics, and hissing with deep and sometimes elongated vowels.
Speakers. Ophiri.
Sounds. Mutters and quickly-spoken, succinct phrases. Sentences are short and to the point.
Speakers. Rogues.
Sounds. Flowy syllables that seem to fit together phonetically regardless of how they are arranged.

Elemental Languages

Elemental languages are spoken by creatures belonging to the appropriate element, except Otherian, which is spoken by Others. Sentients can learn to understand, read, and write them, but speaking them is impossible without arcane aid, either through specially enchanted items, concoctions, spells, or other similar means. It takes 12 cumulative months of study to learn an Elemental language.

Elementals of the quintessential elements – Dark, Light, Space, and Time – do not have languages of their own, but use a specialized form of communication that is akin to a mix between empathy and telepathy. Elementia of these elements have access to this ability as well, but whereas all quintessential elementals can communicate with each other, Dark Elementia can only communicate with Darkened, Light Elementia can only communicate with Enlightened, Space Elementia can only communicate with Displaced, and Time Elementia can only communicate with Temporary.

Speakers. Unliving.
Sounds. Soft, drawn-out, ethereal phonetics that make it difficult to discern vowels from consonants.
Speakers. Drowned.
Sounds. Bubbling, gurgling sounds that seem to consist of fish noises and mostly consonants.
Speakers. Ascended.
Sounds. Soft, billowy phonemes that are akin to voices and whispers on the wind.
Speakers. Scorched.
Sounds. Harsh, crackling, chaotic – some would say hateful – phonetics with pops and bursts accentuating phrases.

Speakers. Fae.

Sounds. Coy, playful, melodious syllables that are easy to confuse with each other; suffice to say it can be quite difficult to discern vowels from consonants.

Speakers. Weightless.
Sounds. An even mix of low, deep and high, lofty sounds that may end or begin suddenly or draw themselves out seemingly unnecessarily.
Speakers. Undead.
Sounds. Low, guttural sounds that tend to end either gradually or suddenly and without warning.
Due to its strange, eldritch, and truly alien nature, Otherian has the potential to drive those who hear it insane. If a creature does not speak or understand Otherian and is not in the process of learning it, they must succeed on a DR 20 Willpower save or gain a new madness for the next 2d12 hours; depending on how badly they fail this save, they may suffer the madness longer, or may be frightened or stunned.
Speakers. Others.
Sounds. Utterly alien and borderline-incomprehensible phonemes.
Speakers. Buried.
Sounds. Harsh, thick, guttural sounds with seemingly no vowels; sounds more like rocks grinding together than anything else.
