Chapter 1: Actions

Characters are able to perform a variety of actions as they explore and interact with the world during their adventures. These actions can be performed in or out of combat, and many can be performed as reactions.

During each round of combat, a creature can perform up to 2 actions on their turn and 1 reaction which can be used during any other creature’s turn. Very rarely, certain special actions may take two or more actions to perform, or certain class features, spells, enchanted items, or other special circumstances may grant a creature more actions or reactions.


When a creature takes this action, they are choosing to attack a creature, character, or object within range using their currently equipped weaponry, their spells, or whatever else they may wish to use. When making an attack with a weapon, unless the weapon has the “DEX” property, add your STR score to the roll.
The number of attacks you can make is altered by your DEX score.
To blind a creature, the blinder must make a DEX check contested against the target’s Reflex save. On a success, the target is blinded until they use an action to remove the blinding element from their eyes.
Whatever weapons a character is holding at any given point during combat are their equipped weapons, and they can take the Attack action with only those weapons unless they choose to swap out one weapon for another. You can change only 1 equipped weapon per action.
To disarm a creature, the disarmer must make a Sleight of Hand or STR check contested by the DEX or STR check of the creature they are attempting to disarm. On a success, their weapon is plucked out of their hand and thrown up to 10 feet away in a direction of the disarmer’s choosing. The weapon can be caught by you or another person in the weapon’s path with a successful DR 15 Sleight of Hand check, provided the being attempting to catch it has an empty hand.
A creature disengages when they wish to leave the melee range of all opponents they are currently in melee range of without provoking any opportunity attacks. If they enter a new opponent’s melee range, they must disengage again if they wish to continue avoiding opportunity attacks.
Evading is a being’s way of preparing for an incoming attack or harmful effect and bracing themselves against it. When a creature takes this action, their Dodge score is doubled and all attacks and damage-dealing effects have disadvantage against them until the start of their next turn; they also have advantage on all Reflex saves for the same period.
When choosing to grapple another creature, the grappler must make a STR or DEX check contested against the Reflex save of the target. On a success, the target is grappled by the grappler, and may take an action on their turn to make a STR or DEX check to free themselves contested against the grappler’s STR or DEX check.
A creature that helps is choosing to aid another character with whatever they are doing. This gives the being they are aiding advantage on whatever they do on their next turn.
The Hide action is used when a creature wishes to avoid being spotted by another entity. In order to hide, a creature needs something to hide behind/in and must make a Stealth check contested by the searching party’s Perception check.
A creature can hold their action if they wish to wait for a specific event to occur in their immediate environment before taking that action, at which point they can take the action as normal. When deciding to hold their action, a player must describe the event that will trigger the desired action, such as an enemy walking through a doorway or an ambush being sprung.
Your maximum jump height in feet is equivalent to your STR score, while your maximum jump length in feet is twice your STR score; with a running start, it becomes double that. A running start requires a character to run uninterrupted for at least 10 feet in a straight line before the jump.
This is arguably the most loosely-definable action, since it can apply to almost infinitely different situations. This action is used when a creature wishes to interact with their environment in such a way that is not otherwise described here; examples include propping a chair up against a door, flipping a table over to use it as cover, or smashing through a glass pane. It is up to the DM whether the action a creature wishes to improvise is doable and, if it is, how difficult it is to do.
You move in a direction of your choosing, to a distance of your choosing, so long as you have enough movement to get there and nothing is in your way. Moving through difficult terrain and moving while prone requires twice as much movement as moving through normal terrain, unless otherwise stated by a special exception. For example, if a character with 30 feet of movement tried to move through difficult terrain, they could only move through 15 feet of it at most.
Additionally, it is very important to note that when a character moves, they do not need to use all of their movement at once, i.e. once they decide to move that action lasts until the end of their turn or they use all of their movement, whichever comes first, e.g. you can move 15 feet, take the Attack action, and then move another 15 feet if you so desired. The amount of movement at your disposal is determined by your AGI score.
Using almost any weapon and/or appropriate item, a creature can pin another to a surface, usually by impaling their clothes to that surface or weighing them down with something heavy. When a creature takes this action, they must make a STR or DEX check (DM’s choice) contested against the target’s Fortitude or Reflex save (DM’s choice). If the pinner wins the contest, the target is considered grappled by whatever is pinning them, and can take an action on their next turn to make a STR check to free themselves. The DR for this check is the result of the pinner’s original STR or DEX check.
To shove a creature, make a STR check contested against their attempted victim’s Fortitude or Reflex save; on a success, they are shoved a number of feet equal to twice the shover’s STR score (minimum 5 feet) in a direction of their choice.
When one creature wishes to speak to another, whether by verbal communication or otherwise, they can do so without using one of their actions, unless the DM rules otherwise.
A creature stands up when they are prone and no longer want to be.
To trip another creature, make a STR or DEX check contested against their Fortitude or Reflex save; on a success, they are knocked prone.
This action is performed whenever a creature wishes to use an item that can be found either in their immediate environment or their inventory. A couple of examples would be quaffing a potion or opening a container. If the item is large and/or would take a large amount of time and/or effort to use, then it may require more than one action to perform.

Reactions

Reactions are performed in immediate response to something in the environment; every creature has at least 1 reaction at their disposal each round, which can be used on any turn other than their own. As stated above, there are some features that add to a character’s list of possible reactions, but those are not described here.


An opportunity attack, or attack of opportunity, can be performed whenever a creature leaves the melee range of another creature, allowing them to take the chance to cause them injury. The Disengage action can be used to avoid attacks of opportunity.
Whenever a character holds an action, they can then perform that action whenever the trigger that they determine is activated.
