Chapter 5: Defenses

Whether you’re defending a caravan from bandits on the road or plundering an ancient lost temple, one thing holds true between adventurers of all levels, backgrounds, and skillsets: a strong offense will only get you so far. Any adventurer hoping to last more than a day or two needs a something to defend themselves in the event things go south. For some, this means being quicker than a bullet with unmatched reflexes. To others, this means thick plates of metal that no Sentient’s sword nor dragon’s tooth could penetrate. And for all, a weak mind will get you about as far as an unstrung bow.

In Iron & Aether, there are 2 primary forms of defense that an adventurer has at their disposal: their Dodge, which is a quantification of how well your character can move out of danger’s way, and their Armor, which details how much the attacks that do hit them get blocked by what they’re wearing (their Fortitude and Willpower are also used to protect them from poison and psychic damage respectively). Your Armor and Dodge scores combined form your Defense (DEF) and Ranged Defense (RaDEF) scores.

Defense and Ranged Defense

The Defense (DEF) of a creature is a number that tells the player and the DM how much damage is reduced from incoming melee attacks, and is determined by adding the creature’s Armor and Dodge scores; the lowest a creature’s DEF score can be is 0. A creature’s Ranged Defense (RaDEF) functions very similarly, except that it is applied to any and all attacks made at range, including explosives, ranged spells, traps, and the like. RaDEF is determined by adding a creature’s DEF score to the RaDEF bonus they get from their Size.

To find out how much damage has been dealt to a creature, take the result of the attack roll and subtract the target’s DEF or RaDEF score. This damage is referred to as the final damage of the attack.

Armor

A character’s Armor score tells them how well-defended they are from attacks that connect with their physical bodies; a low Armor score means that the gear your character is wearing hardly protects them, while a high Armor score means that they are quite well-defended, likely wearing thick plates of steel or something enchanted. In short, the higher your Armor score, the less damage you’ll be taking; however, heavier armor will weigh you down and restrain your Dodge score, as well as make it more difficult to travel on foot (refer to Part 5: Equipment, Chapter 3: Armor for more information).

Your Armor score is comprised of your heritage and the actual armor you are wearing on your body; the lowest a Sentient’s Armor score can be is 1.

Dodge

Your Dodge score - separate from the Dodge Penalty determined by your Size - quantifies your character’s ability to avoid immediate danger: it is a collection of their reflexes, defensive agility, instinct for danger, and, to a certain extent, their hand-eye coordination. A high Dodge score means that hardly anything, from a flying arrow to a swung blade, will get anywhere near you. The higher your Dodge score is, the less harm will touch you; however, heavy armor and equipment will reduce and constrain your Dodge score. The lowest a creature’s Dodge score can be is 0.

Your Dodge score is determined by your Size and your AGI and DEX modifiers, and is directly modified by the Armor you are wearing:

Light, Medium, or no armor: Dodge = size penalty + AGI score + DEX score;

Heavy armor: Dodge = size penalty + AGI score.

Fortitude and Willpower

In the next chapter, another type of defense is covered: saves. Two of these saves, Fortitude and Willpower, also function as forms of defense against poison and psychic damage respectively.

Resistances and Vulnerabilities

Some creatures have resistances and vulnerabilities to certain types of damage; this just means that they take more or less damage from an attack that deals damage of that type. If a creature is resistant to an attack’s damage, then the final damage dealt to them by that attack is halved (rounded down). If a creature is vulnerable to an attack’s damage, then the final damage dealt to them by that attack is doubled.
