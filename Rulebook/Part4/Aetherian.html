Aetherian

Becoming an Aetherian

How exactly Sentients become Aetherians varies from world to world, and at the end of the day, it is up to your GM to decide. In the world of Aethra for example, the primary method of becoming an Aetherian is to ingest or inject Aether, and after suffering through the ensuing Aether Sickness, there is a chance the sufferer will come out of it an Aetherian. Other methods include being born on the appropriate plane, finding a relic that grants you Aetherian powers, being blessed with powers by one of the gods, or simply being born one. No one method of becoming an Aetherian is incorrect, unless it runs directly contradictory to the rules of the world that your GM has established; if your GM says that in their world, Aetherians are born with their powers, then you cannot make an Aetherian that learns their spells through tireless study (unless they allow it).

Control Over the Elements

The Aetherian is a special class: it does not have any class feats, but instead there are 12 different sub-types of the overarching class, referred to here as the Elemental Domains. It is also one of two classes with access to spells, and because of this has access to some of the most unique, powerful, and useful abilities in the game. An Aetherian can only ever have control over one Elemental Domain, unless they gain access to another through an external source such as an enchant.

The Power of Aether

Like the substance Aetherians' get their abilities from, the term Aether also refers to the spells they cast, the mystical abilities that they wield, the powers at their command, and more. Each Aetherian’s Aether is tied to a specific Elemental Domain, which they gain control over at the time of their transformation into an Aetherian, described in the “Becoming an Aetherian” section above. Each Elemental Domain is mostly restricted to its own abilities and powers due to their inherent differences, however there are some common spells, called such and detailed at the end of the class description.

Class Features

As an Aetherian you gain the following class features.

Health

Health at 1st Level: 6 + your CON score

Health Beyond 1st Level: 1d6 (or 3) + your CON score per Aetherian level after 1st

Proficiencies

Skills at 1st Level: You gain 1 proficiency in Arcana.

Skills Beyond 1st Level: You gain 1 SP every time you level up and 1 WP every 4 levels starting at level 4.


Aether

At 1st level, you command one of the 12 elements, using that power to cast spells and create arcane effects at your will. These abilities are collectively referred to as Aether, named after the mystical substance from which you have gained your powers.

The damage dealt by your spells is determined by your proficiency with your Aether, represented by your Aether Die (AD), which starts as a d6 but becomes a d8 at 11th level. Several class features make use of the Aether Die, mostly for damage but occasionally for other effects.

Lastly, casting spells and using Aether usually requires gesticulations and fine movements, meaning that you cannot cast spells or use class features while wearing any armor that grants disadvantage on DEX checks or shields.

Aether Save DR

Several elemental effects cause the target(s) to make a save against one or more conditions of the effect, whether that be getting pushed, keeping their thoughts shielded, retaining control of their body, or something else entirely. The DR for these saves is referred to as your Aether Save DR, and is determined as follows:

Aether Save DR: 10 + ½ the maximum value of your Aether Die + your Aetherian level

Elemental Domain

At 1st level, you have discovered your inherent control over one of the 12 elements: Air, Earth, Fire, Water, Death, Gravity, Life, Magic, Dark, Light, Space, or Time. These Domains have different abilities, spells, and roles both in and out of combat; additionally, each Domain gets additional features at 5th and 13th levels. Whichever Domain your character controls is up to you, but you should also consider how this effects their personality, their background, how they came across their powers, their struggle to control the Aether they now command. Perhaps your Pyromancer is hot-headed and quick to anger, or your Magician is coy and mischievous. Whatever the case may be, be sure to put some thought into how discovering, gaining, struggling with, and controlling their Domain has influenced your Aetherian’s life, background, and personality.


Elemancy

As an inherent part of their domain, Aetherians are able to do an incalculable number of simple effects relating to their element without expending any Mana, such as creating puffs of air, minor sensory effects, or dousing Tiny fires. If your Aetherian wishes to perform a simple elemental effect, they can do so without expending Mana. This effect can deal up to 2 AD worth of damage before it costs Mana.


Mana

Mana is a numeric representation of an Aetherian’s mental fortitude, their remaining strength and will to wield the Aether at their disposal. It determines how much you can use your Aether before needing to rest and what spells you are able to cast.

Your primary method of regaining Mana is resting; a quick rest will restore 1 AD Mana, while a night's or day's rest always restores your Mana to full. Alternatively, Ethers restore Mana similar to how Potions restore Health. You also regain 10 Mana whenever you ingest ½ a pound of Aether of your domain’s type.


Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one of your ability scores by 1, two of them by .5, or take a classless feat. You cannot increase an ability score beyond 5 via this feature.


Enhanced Element

At 11th level, you have mastered your element enough that you have learned to control a more advanced form of it. The enhanced elements are as follows:

Air. Aeromancers are able to mix in trace amounts of ice and fire into their abilities, and as such, are able to change the damage type of their spells. Mixing in shards of ice changes the damage type to physical/freeze and sparks of fire to burn.

Earth. Terramancers are able to manipulate metal and glass to their wills just as easily as they are able to control raw earth or stone.

Fire. Pyromancers find themselves able to control lightning as well as fire. This allows them to change their flame spells to lightning and vice versa, which in turn changes the damage they deal from burn to electric.

Water. Aquamancers gain the ability to tamper with their water, making it poisonous or acidic at their discretion.

Death. The element of Death is final, and cannot be improved or altered. However, advanced Necromancers have learned the ins and outs of Death enough that they begin to learn about its complement, Life. Select 4 Life spells that cost < 6 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Gravity. At its strongest, gravity can bend not just matter to its will, but Dark, Light, Space, and Time as well, bending and warping them in ways none of the other Ethereal elements can. Select 2 Dark spells, 2 Light spells, 2 Space spells, and 2 Time spells that each cost < 3 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Life. Animancers, being greatly proficient with the element of souls, begin to understand what it is that ends them, that converse element that is so feared, Death. Select 4 Death spells that cost < 6 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Magic. The mysticism of magic is, by its very nature strange, ever-changing, and incapable of being fully understood, at least by Sentient minds. How, then, is such an element enhanced? In a way, it isn’t; it is simply altered. Lie becomes truth, falsity becomes reality, and illusion, through the power, training, and will of the Magician, becomes conjuration – though almost always temporarily.

Dark. Umbramancers and Luxomancers both dabble in the mirror of their element, infusing their Aether with a bit of light to create twilight. This allows them to learn a few spells of the opposing element, as well as alter their own spells to some degree. Select 4 Light spells that cost < 6 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Light. Luxomancers, like their Umbramancer brethren, dip their toes in the reflection of their element, infusing their powers with a touch of dark to create twilight. This grants them knowledge of a few of the opposite element’s spells, as well as affect their own spells a little bit. Select 4 Dark spells that cost < 6 Mana; you can now cast those spells as though they were your own. These spells are permanent and cannot be changed.

Space. The element of Space is seemingly the most orderly one: everything that physically exists seems to make sense, seems to belong where it is, it geometrically fits into the world – unless a particularly strong Mattemancer wishes it not so. Powerful Mattemancers can force the geometry of the world to obey their own will, making the laws of physics into something more akin to guidelines than actual rules.

Time. Time is an unceasing river that flows ever forward, and Chronomancers are able to but only dip their hands into the water. Strong Chronomancers, however, can use their slight bit of sway and control to cease or even ever so slightly reverse the river’s flow for just a moment.


Elemental Tongue

At 14th level, you learn and are able to speak and write the language of your element: Air learns Angelic, Earth learns Terran, Fire learns Demonic, Water learns Aquan, Death learns Mortuan, Gravity learns Gravician, Life learns Animan, and Magic learns Faen.

Dark, Light, Space, and Time do not learn a specific language, but instead learn a complex and indescribable array of empathetic and telepathic emotions and thoughts that can be used to communicate with any and all of the Darkened, Displaced, Enlightened, and Timeless.


Favored Spell

At 17th level, every Aetherian has a single simple spell permanently embedded in their mind, a favorite of theirs that they’re used to calling on and can cast with hardly any effort. Select a single spell that costs < 3 Mana; from this point on, you can cast that spell without expending Mana.


One With the Element

When you reach 20th level, you are truly a master of your element, nearly a demigod with nigh-unmatched arcane power. From now on, whenever standing in an area heavily saturated with your element or on your domain’s plane, you regain 1d12 Health and 1 Mana per minute. An example for a suitable environment for each domain is given here:

Air: An area of intense natural wind, or being in freefall; Earth: Making physical contact with soil that has not left the world’s surface; Fire: Making physical contact with natural fire; Water: Making physical contact with natural water; Death: A graveyard, or within 5 feet of someone who has died within the past ½ hour; Gravity: Increasing your weight to thrice its norm; Life: An area where life thrives, or within 5 feet of a Sentient that has been born or resurrected within the last day; Magic: Standing within 5 feet of anything arcane that is not one of your spells or effects; Dark: An area of complete natural darkness; Light: An area of natural bright light; Space: Making physical contact with at least 1 pound of any form of Aether; Time: Standing within a flowing river.


Elemental Domains

Every Aetherian discovers that they have control over a single natural element: Air, Earth, Fire, Water, Death, Gravity, Life, Magic, Dark, Light, Space, or Time. Each element, with a couple of exceptions, has its own powers, abilities, and spells.

Air

Aeromancers are able to push and pull the air around them in order to manipulate it in accordance with their whims. More powerful Aeromancers are able to mix in trace amounts of fire and ice to amplify their abilities.


Aeromancy

Since Aeromancers command the winds, their Elemancy centers around the command of wind and air.

Enhanced Aeromancy. At 11th level, Aeromancers can infuse bits of fire and ice into their air currents, allowing them to perform the simplest acts of Pyro/Aquamancy and deal burn and freeze damage.


Aerial Finesse

At 5th level, you cannot unwillingly be pushed or affected by winds or air currents. Additionally, whenever you take fall damage, you can reduce the damage you take by 2 AD.


Grace of the Wind

Once you hit 13th level, your command of the air and winds around you has increased enough to grant you significantly more protection from the harmful forces around you. From here on out, your Dodge score is increased by an amount equal to ¼ your Aetherian level (rounded up if half), and whenever you take fall damage, the damage you take is reduced by an amount equal to twice your Aetherian level.

Pyromancers are able to control fire and bend it to their will with ease. Pyromancers can superheat the air around them at will to create the fire they need for their various spells and features, or they can choose to manipulate an existing source of fire nearby. Pyromancy

Pyromancy is all about super-heating the air and creating great and powerful bursts of intense flames.

Enhanced Pyromancy. Upon reaching 11th level, Pyromancers gain control over lightning, vastly improving their pyromantic repertoire.


Burning Rage

At 5th level, Pyromancers are able to allow the heat of the fire they control to aid them in their struggles. From here on out, you can use an action to enter a state of fiery rage that lasts for the next minute. During this time, anyone who touches you or strikes you with a melee weapon within 5 feet of you takes 2 AD burn damage. You can do this twice between rests.


Scorching Rage

When you reach 13th level, your fiery hot rage gains some bonuses. The first is that it now deals 3 AD damage; the second is that it can now deal burn or electric damage at your discretion; and the third is that you can now do it four times between rests.

Death

The element of the end, the great equalizer, the great unknown, that which comes for every Sentient, this is the element that Necromancers (colloquially called Morticians) command. Where others find fear in Death, Necromancers find power, power that they use to great and mortal effect. More powerful Necromancers are able to incorporate the power of Life into their Aether.


Necromancy

Necromancers call Death home, and bend it – and the strange and morbid powers of undeath – to their will.

Enhanced Necromancy. Starting at 11th level, Necromancers begin to learn the intricacies of Life and how close it truly is to Death, allowing them to perform simple feats of Animancy and learn some Animancer spells.


Foot in the Grave

At 5th level you gain resistance to rot and poison damage, and you now have advantage on saves against poisons, diseases, and being sickened.


Death Sense

Starting at 13th level, your closeness with death clues you in to when someone dies in close physical proximity of you: you now automatically sense when someone dies within a distance of 10 times your Aetherian level feet from you. You do not know who or how they died.

Gravity

Gravimancers control the element of pushing, pulling, and holding – particularly, holding the universe together. Without Gravity, the world as Sentients know it would collapse in and out of itself, falling apart entirely. More powerful Gravimancers are able to bend a fraction of the Quintessential Elements to their will as well as typical gravity.


Gravimancy

The element of Gravity is fairly simple, and is primarily concerned with the pushing and pulling of creatures and objects.

Enhanced Gravimancy. Truly powerful Gravity affects not just objects and creatures, but the fundamentals of the universe themselves: Dark, Light, Space, and Time. This allows a Gravimancer to perform the simplest of Umbramantic, Luxomantic, Mattemantic, and Chronomantic feats, as well as learn a couple of spells from each Domain.


Flight

When you reach 5th level, you have strong enough control over gravity and its effects on you to permanently grant yourself a flying speed in feet equal to thrice your Aetherian level.


Center of Gravity

At 13th level, your ability to resist the push and pull of external forces extends beyond that of just gravity. You can no longer be pushed or pulled by any natural non-arcane force (such as rushing water or powerful winds) unless you allow it. Creatures, however, are still able to move you against your will, assuming they are strong enough.

Life

Animancers are able to tap into the very life force of their targets and use their own energies to hurt or heal them. More powerful Animancers are able to borrow from Death to end life.


Animancy

The element of Life is primarily concerned with restoration, but on the other hand, Life can also be cruel and punishing.

Enhanced Animancy. Because Life and Death are intrinsically and forever intertwined, Life learns to imitate the simplest of Necromantic feats just as Necromancers learn to do the same with Animancy.


Cure

At 5th level your natural healing abilities strengthen as your powers continue to grow. When you target a creature using your Elemental Strike, you can choose to heal them instead of hurt them; if you choose to do this, the target does not reduce the amount of healing by their RaDEF score and is healed for only 2 AD instead of 3. Doing this costs 1 Mana.


Heal

When you reach 13th level, whenever you target an ally with your Cure feature, their Health is restored by 4 AD.

Magic

When Sentients think of Magic, they also think of mysticism, illusion, imagination, and the Fae, with their mysterious tricks and troublesome pranks. Because of this thaumaturgy, Magic, more than any other element, is associated with lies and deceit, and Magimancers – also known as Magicians – are no strangers to embracing that reputation. More powerful Magicians are able to turn their illusions into solid structures, for a brief time.


Magimancy

Magic is the element of illusion, wonder, and imagination, granting Magicians abilities in that vein, which usually take the form of illusions of all kinds.

Enhanced Magimancy. Magicians learn to find the truth within the lie, and in so doing learn how to make their illusions solid for a minute or less.


Truth and Lies

At 5th level, your affinity with Magic and the ways of the Fae clue you in to the true nature of things. You automatically see through illusions (though you are aware of their presence), and your Beguilement, Insight, and Perception proficiencies increase by 1, all of which can now be increased to 6.


Duplicity

Your talent with illusions only grows. Starting from 13th level, you can use an action to create a perfect illusory duplicate of yourself (including your equipment) in an unoccupied space that you can see within 3 times your Aetherian level feet of you that lasts for 1 minute, until you dismiss it, or until you lose concentration. You can create it a number of times between rests equal to ¼ your Aetherian level (rounded up if half). A creature can see through this illusion by using an action to make a Perception check against your Aether Save DR; if they attempt to interact with it physically (such as by trying to touch it) they will immediately notice that it is illusory when this fails.

On your turn, you can mentally command the duplicate to perform up to two actions without using an action; should you command it to “perform” an action other than movement, the action will not actually be performed (you can, however, command the illusion to speak with your voice). You could, for instance, tell the illusion to move 15 feet and “attack” a creature, however the illusion will not do any damage, and the illusion may be revealed for what it is.

If you and your duplicate are in adjacent spaces or otherwise side-by-side, any attack against you has a 50% chance of being aimed at the illusion until or unless the attacker realizes the illusion’s true nature. Likewise, if you and your illusion are attacking the same creature, all of your attacks have advantage so long as the target is unaware of the illusory nature of your duplicate.

Dark

Umbramancers command and bend the element of Dark, the element of silence, secrets, chaos, and freedom. More powerful Umbramancers learn to infuse a bit of Light into their Aether to create twilight.


Umbramancy

That which dwells in the dark, as well as the darkness itself, are under the control of Umbramancers.

Enhanced Umbramancy. When they reach 11th level, Umbramancers learn to incorporate motes of Light into their Dark, creating twilight and allowing them to perform simple Luxomancy.


Emissary of Night

At 5th level, your attunement to the dark grants you special arcane sight. You now see normally in dim light, darkness, and arcane darkness, and have advantage on saves against being blinded.


Avatar of the Void

At 13th level you can feel the warmth of the darkness on your skin, feel its power pumping through your veins. Space

The element of Space is that of matter and material, of that which physically exists and takes up, well, space. It is orderly, stable, dependable, and, as one of the Quintessential elements, quite powerful, with unique abilities. More powerful Mattemancers are able to break the laws of physics with their Aether, as well as create new demiplanes consisting of their own Space.


Mattemancy

Mattemancers technically take command over all of space, and their conjurations usually take the form of crystalline structures, though this does not necessarily need to be the case.

Enhanced Mattemancy. Advanced Mattemancy deals not just with manipulating space, but undoing and creating it, allowing Mattemancers to disintegrate creatures and objects or create pocket dimensions big enough to suit their needs.


Minor Transmutation

Your control over the raw material of the universe allows you to manipulate it to your will. At 5th level, you can take 1 minute and an amount of material in your possession worth < your Aetherian level in Aeons and that takes up < ¼ your Aetherian level in cubic feet and transmute it into any other item with the same volume, mass, and state of matter. The item cannot be enchanted or a concoction, and cannot be worth more than the given restriction; if you attempt to create a valuable item, such as a precious gem, you manage to create only a fake, and an Appraisal check against your Aether Save DR will reveal it as such.


Major Transmutation

From 13th level onward, your power over Space only grows, allowing your transmutations to become much more powerful. The material you transmute can now take up an amount of cubic feet equal to < ½ your Aetherian level, and you can now transform one state of matter into another (not including plasma). It no longer needs to be in your possession, and can instead be a number of feet from you equal to your Aetherian level. Lastly, it takes only 2 actions instead of 1 minute. All other restrictions still apply.

Time

Time is the element of the unceasing, ever-flowing river that ensures everything is constantly moving forward and that a cause will always have its effect. Typically, Time only ever moves forward; however, stronger Chronomancers are able to stop it entirely, or even reverse it just for a moment.


Chronomancy

To control and alter the flow of time is the domain of Chronomancy; while this usually results in slowing events down or speeding creatures up, Chronomancers can also conjure shifting sands or flowing waters to exert physical influence.

Enhanced Chronomancy. Advanced Time control allows one to not just speed up or slow down the passage of time, but also rewind it just a bit.


Time Exemption

At 5th level, your affinity with Time keeps you from feeling its more negative effects. You can no longer be slowed at all, nor aged via arcane means. Additionally, you now age at a rate equal to 1/your Aetherian level.


Controlled Chronology

When you reach 13th level, whenever you rewind time by any means, you can choose for the condition and/or position of your body to remain the same as it was before the rewind. You can do this twice between rests; you can give one of your uses of this feature to another creature that you can touch, and it lasts until their next rest.
