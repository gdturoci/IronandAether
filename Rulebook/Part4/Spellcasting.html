Spellcasting

Spells

A spell is a special arcane effect produced by the power of the caster; in the case of Aetherians, it is via their Aether, while for Otherworldly Arcanists, it is through their knowledge of the eldritch otherworldly forces that permeate the world, and their subsequent ability to manipulate those forces to achieve their goals. Additionally, there are also enchanted items that are able to recreate the effects of spells, such as wands and staves. Spells usually produce some sort of visible and immediately noticeable effect, though there are certainly exceptions to this.

Perceiving a Spell Being Cast

Aetherian Spells. All Aetherian spells require movement of some kind, usually some sort of somatic allegory for what the spell is trying to do, e.g. when casting the spell Burst an Aeromancer might forcefully push their hands out in a mimicry of what the spell is doing, or a Chronomancer might use their hands to wind back the arms of a spectral clock when casting Rewind. Because of these movements, an Aetherian cannot cast spells while wearing Medium or Heavy armor (except chain shirts) or Medium or larger shields. If an Aetherian wishes to cast a spell subtly so as not to be noticed doing so, they can make a Sleight of Hand check contested against the other party’s Perception check.

Aetherian Spellcasting

Spell Traits

Every Aetherian spell has 8 traits which detail specific and important aspects of the spell: Mana Cost, Casting Time, Range, Damage, Save, Duration, Area of Effect, and Notable Effects. Aetherians can expend extra Mana to improve these traits, the rules for which are covered further on.

Mana Cost (M.C.). The amount of Mana the caster must expend to unleash the spell’s power; the caster’s maximum Mana must be > to the Mana Cost of a spell for them to be able to cast it at all. When casting a spell, the caster must subtract the Mana Cost of the spell from their remaining Mana; if the Mana Cost of the spell is > their remaining Mana, they can still cast it, however their Mana will be reduced to 0 and their current Health will be depleted by 5 times the remaining difference.

Casting Time (C.T.). How long it takes for the spell to be cast. If a spell has a casting time of more than 1 action, then the caster cannot take any other actions while casting or the casting will fail. Until the casting is complete, the caster is considered to be concentrating (see Duration below) on the spell.

Range (Rng.). How far away from the caster’s current position the origin point of the spell can be placed/the target(s) can be; the caster must have line of sight on the origin point/target in order to cast the spell. If the caster has line of sight but cannot actually see the origin point/target, then the damage of the spell may be rolled with disadvantage, or if the spell requires a save, the target can make it with advantage.

Damage (Dam.). The type of damage (if any) a spell deals; spells always deal 3 Aether Dice of damage unless otherwise stated. The caster is immune to damage from their own spells.

Save. What type of save, if any, is needed; saves are always made against your Aether Save DR. The effects of failing the save will be given in Notable Effects. At the cost of 2 additional Mana, the caster can give one of the targets disadvantage on their save(s).

Duration (Dur.). How long the spell can last. Spells with the Concentration tag as part of their duration require the caster’s constant focus, and being attacked can cause the caster to lose focus, ending the spell prematurely. If a caster takes damage while casting a spell with the concentration tag, they must make a Willpower save against twice the final damage dealt; on a failure, the spell ends. A caster can have only one concentration spell going at a time.

Area of Effect (A.o.E.). Detailed below.

Notable Effects (Efcs.). What will happen inside the spell’s AoE/to its targets.

Areas of Effect

A spell’s Area of Effect (AoE) gives the shape of the spell, as well as the dimensions of that shape. Each shape has a point of origin, or a spot from which the spell’s power and energy erupts. The direction into which it erupts is usually up to the caster, though some spells default to the direction the caster is facing.

The shape and dimensions of a spell’s AoE is specified in its description, and the different possible shapes (and their respective points of origin) are given here:

  • A cone has length and width and the point of origin is at the tip;
  • A cube has the length of the faces and the point of origin is in the middle of one face;
  • A cylinder has diameter and length and the point of origin is on one face;
  • A line has length (and sometimes height), is assumed to be 5 feet wide, and the point of origin is at one end;
  • And a sphere has diameter and the point of origin is in the exact center.

Instead of a shape, a spell may specify a number of targets for the spell to affect. In this case, the spell’s description will specify what the targets must be and how many of them. A creature will usually not know it has been targeted unless the spell produces some sort of perceivable effect, though some spells, such as an Animancer’s Read Thoughts, are not typically perceivable unless the spell’s description says otherwise.

Improving Spells

Spells can be improved at the cost of additional Mana: typically, a spell’s Range, Damage, Duration, and AoE are what can be improved. Some spells are exceptions to the guidelines given here and will specify in their descriptions if that is the case.

Range. If a spell does not have a range of “Touch” or “Self”, then its range can be extended by 10 feet for every 1 extra Mana spent.

Damage. A spell’s damage can be improved at the cost of 1 Mana per additional Aether Die.

Duration. Improving spell duration is abnormal, and as such is given in table form; all improvements cost 1 Mana. When extending a spell’s duration, the caster must expend the additional Mana at the time they extend the duration, e.g. to extend a spell that lasts 1 minute past its original duration, 1 Mana must be spent at the end of the minute, automatically extending the spell for another 2 rounds (unless they choose to end it early).

Area of Effect. To extend a spell’s AoE by 5 feet in a single dimension (such as making a cone 5 feet wider or a line 5 feet longer) costs 1 Mana. For target spells, additional targets can be selected at the cost of 2 Mana per target or the Mana Cost of the spell, whichever is lower.

Enhanced Element

Many Aetherian spells benefit from the Enhanced Element feature that they gain at level 11. If a spell has the EE Optional tag in its description, the caster can make use of their Enhanced Element feature to enhance the spell’s abilities (usually just altering the spell’s damage type); if a spell has the EE Required tag, the caster cannot cast the spell until they reach level 11; a spell with neither cannot be modified with the Enhanced Element feature at all.

Spell Traps

Most any spell can be set as a trap. When it is, the spell becomes a glyph, or a circular collection of arcane runes, symbols, and energy that faintly glow with the spell’s power and which must be < 3 feet or > 6 inches in diameter. A glyph cannot be disarmed or dispelled by any ordinary means, though when set off, the energy of the glyph is expended and it disappears. If a spell set as a trap has a range, it targets itself, and if it has a direction for the AoE, that direction is the direction the glyph is facing.

There are 5 steps to setting a spell as a trap:

  1. The caster casts the spell as normal;
  2. They spend an additional amount of Mana equal to half the Mana spent on the spell, rounded down;
  3. They designate a surface or small object that they can touch to embed the power and effects of the spell into;
  4. They spend the casting time of the spell imbuing the spell’s power into that surface or object;
  5. They set the conditions for the spell trap to trigger, e.g. touching the spell glyph, moving the object the glyph is placed on, opening the book the glyph was placed in, etc. All spell traps have the following condition: if the glyph is moved 10 feet from where it was placed, the spell goes off.

To spot a glyph, a creature can make an Arcana or Perception check against what the caster’s Aether Save DR was at the time they made the glyph, and to determine what spell the glyph contains, the creature can either be the same type of Aetherian as the one who set the trap or succeed on an Arcana check against what the caster’s Aether Save DR was at the time they set the trap.
