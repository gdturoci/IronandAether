Psychokinetic

The Power of the Mind

Psychokinetics manipulate objects, energies, and even the minds of those around them through disciplined practice, study, and meditation. Through their own minds, they are able to bend and shape reality to their will, from throwing objects about the room to hypnotizing their enemies into fighting for them.

Most Psychokinetics achieve their power through sheer study, practice, and force of will, usually training underneath a powerful and accomplished Psychokinetic looking to pass their skills on. Others are born naturals at it, finding themselves able to pick up toys several feet away as toddlers or sense the emotions of those around them before even reaching adolescence.

Class Features

As a Psychokinetic you gain the following class features.

Health

Health at 1st Level: 6 + your CON score

Health Beyond 1st Level: 1d6 (or 3) + your CON score per Psychokinetic level after 1st

Proficiencies

Skills at 1st Level: You gain 1 WP to place in any Weapon Skill of your choice.

Skills Beyond 1st Level: You gain 1 SP every time you level up and 1 WP at every even-numbered level.


Energy Manipulation

All Psychokinetics are able to exert a degree of influence over the energies in their immediate environment, either increasing or decreasing the raw power of the forces around them. For the purposes of this feature, you gain 1 proficiency in an Exotic weapon henceforth referred to as “Energy”, which you can expend WP on to increase your proficiency with it just as you would with any other Exotic weapon.

Once per round, whenever an attack is made within 30 feet of you that deals burn, electric, freeze, physical, or sonic damage, you can use your reaction to roll a single d6 and increase or decrease the damage dealt by that attack; the attack can also be made by you, in which case increasing/decreasing the damage dealt is considered part of your attack and does not use your reaction. As your proficiency with Energy increases, the number of d6s you roll increases proportionately, just like with normal weapon attacks.

Because of the fine movements required, you cannot use your Energy while wearing any armor that grants disadvantage on DEX checks or Medium or Large shields.

Psycho Save DR

Certain Psychokinetic features require a creature to make a save against something you do. This save is determined via your INT score, since your abilities are derived from your understanding and knowledge of the forces in the world around you.

Psycho Save DR = your Psychokinetic level + triple your INT score (rounded down)


Class Feats

Once you hit 2nd level, the power within your mind has grown enough that you have learned to enhance it with special abilities. At the end of the class description, under “Class Feats”, you may select one Novice class feat.

At 6th level, you may select either one Journeyman or two Novice class feats.

When you reach 11th level, you may select either one Expert, one Journeyman and one Novice, or three Novice class feats.

At 15th level your psychic powers are near-legendary, and you may now select one Master, one Expert and one Novice, two Journeyman, one Journeyman and two Novice, or four Novice class feats.

If a feat has any prerequisites, you must meet those prerequisites before you can take it. Feats cannot be taken more than once unless they explicitly say so. Your feat choices are permanent and cannot be changed.


Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one of your ability scores by 1, two of them by .5, or take a classless feat. You cannot increase an ability score beyond 5 via this feature.


Entropic Redirection

At 5th level, you have learned not just to increase or decrease the energies around you, but also how to manipulate them for your own purposes, turning the destructive powers of your enemies against them. From now on, whenever you use Energy to decrease damage, you can then store and redirect that damage onto another creature or object within range.

To do this, once you use Energy to reduce damage from an attack, record the amount and type of damage that you reduce. You can then use an action to make an Energy attack against another creature or object that you can see within 30 feet of you. Any final damage that creature or object takes from this attack is equal to the amount and type of damage that you stored. Any energy that you have stored lasts a number of minutes equal to your Energy proficiency, and at the end of that time the energy is lost.

As an example, let’s say you store 14 points of burn damage from an attack directed at you; on your next turn, you decide that you would like to inflict this damage on another creature. Assuming you have 3 proficiency in Energy, you would then roll 3d6, and if the result is > the target’s DEF score, the target immediately takes 14 points of burn damage, regardless of what the result of the Energy attack was.

Creative Redirection. Consider how you can use your Entropic Redirection feature creatively, or what outside-the-box uses it might have. For example, if you store freeze damage, perhaps you can redirect it into water to freeze the water? Or if you store electric damage and make need to jump-start an electric machine? Work with your DM to see what creative uses you can find for this feature.


Improved Energy Manipulation

By the time you have reached 9th level, your ability to manipulate the energies in your immediate environment has greatly improved. From here on out, your Energy gains the following benefits:

  • Its range is extended to 60 feet;
  • whenever you roll to increase or decrease the amount of damage dealt by an attack, you can now add your INT score to the roll;
  • you can now increase your Energy proficiency to 6.


Improved Entropic Redirection

Once you hit 14th level, you have become adept enough at redirecting the energies of your environment that you have learned to enhance them with your own abilities. From now on, your Entropic Redirection has the following benefits:

  • You can now add your INT score to Energy attacks when using Entropic Redirection;
  • you can now add your INT to the damage dealt by Entropic Redirection;
  • enemies are no longer resistant to damage that you redirect if they normally would be, and are instead resistant if they would normally be immune.


Superior Energy and Entropy

At 20th level, your Energy gains the following benefits:

  • Its range is extended to 120 feet;
  • its base damage die is now 1d8; ergo, if you have 3 proficiency in Energy, you roll 3d8 whenever you use Energy Manipulation or Entropic Redirection.

Psychokinetic Class Feats

Psychokinetics learn to focus their practices into a myriad of abilities, from energy blades to telekinesis to telepathy. These abilities are presented here as class feats, which may be chosen at levels 2, 6, 11, and 15. However they choose to enhance their powers, every Psychokinetic is an incredible powerhouse and an impressive force to be reckoned with.

Novice


You have learned to project your mind outside of your body, allowing you to perceive events and locations that you are physically separate from. Using two actions, you can now see and hear anything up to 30 feet away from a point you select within 1 mile that you do not have to see. If you are personally familiar with a creature or object, you can use this feat to locate and spy on them and their immediate surroundings.
Prerequisite: Level 6
You’ve gotten better at storing any energy that you’ve redirected, allowing you to keep any stored damage for twice as long.
Prerequisite: Psychic Armament
Whenever you conjure a weapon of psychic energy, it no longer takes an action.
With this feat, you can use an action to conjure a weapon of pure psychic energy. This weapon can take the shape of any weapon except Trick weapons. Damage with this weapon uses your proficiency with that weapon’s type, e.g. if you summon a dagger and have 3 proficiency in Small weapons, that dagger deals 3d4 + your STR/DEX physical damage. If you conjure a weapon that requires ammunition, such as a bow, you can summon up to 10 pieces of ammunition, and must use an additional action to summon up to 10 more; you can have up to 20 extra pieces of ammunition in this way (physical ammunition can still be used with your conjured weapon). Should you summon a repeater or revolver, you summon 6 extra bullets and can keep up to 12 extra bullets. The weapon is purple, translucent, and lasts until you dismiss it. Only you can wield this weapon, and it disappears whenever you lose physical contact with it (except ammunition, which disappears after it hits or misses).
Taking this feat allows you to manipulate the matter in your environment using only your mind. Using this feature, you are able to move as many objects within 30 feet of you that you can see as you wish, so long as their combined weight in pounds does not exceed 25 times your Psychokinetic level. You cannot lift living creatures or sentient constructs, including yourself, nor can you use this feature while wearing armor or shields that impose disadvantage on DEX checks due to the fine movements required.
If the object you are trying to manipulate is currently being worn or carried by another creature, they must make a STR check against your INT check to resist having the object pulled away from them.
Lastly, whenever you make a STR or DEX check or perform an Action Skill that would normally require the use of your STR or DEX, you can instead choose to use your INT instead as you manipulate an object telekinetically rather than manually, so long as it makes sense for you to be able to do so; for example, you could make a STR or Sleight of Hand check this way, but not a Swim or Stealth check.
When manipulating objects using your Telekinesis feature, there are 2 types of manipulation you can perform on an object at any one time: Fine Control and Throw, which are explained here:
Fine Control. Fine control is used whenever you perform simple actions with an object you are holding using your Telekinesis feature. Out-of-combat examples include using a key on a lock, gently moving or rotating an object in such a way that does not deal damage, opening doors, etc. During combat, you can exert fine control on an object as if you were wielding it as a melee weapon. Attacks are mechanically the same as normal, however instead of your STR or DEX, you must now use your INT. Additionally, your INT score grants you more attacks when wielding weapons telekinetically in much the same way your DEX score would when wielding them normally.
Though you can hold more than one object at a time using your Telekinesis feature, you can only exert fine control over one object at a time. You cannot exert fine control over an object in any way outside of the range of this feature (30 feet at this level).
Throw. Throwing an object simply means you are using an action to launch it up to a distance equivalent to the range of your Telekinesis - even if it goes beyond the range of your Telekinesis - in a direction of your choosing. The damage dealt by an object that you throw is the same as the damage it would normally deal (scaling with your Throwing proficiency), however instead of using your STR or DEX, you use your INT. The number of objects you can throw using a single action is the same as the number of attacks you can make using this feature.
At Higher Levels. The range and weight limit of this feature increases as you level up: at 6th level, they become 60 feet and 50 times your Psychokinetic level, respectively; at 11th level, they increase to 90 feet and 75 times your Psychokinetic level; and at 15th level, they become 120 feet and 100 times your Psychokinetic level.
Prerequisite: Telekinesis
You have learned to use your Telekinesis to better protect yourself, such as by moving enemies’ attacks aside or cloaking yourself in a thin field of kinetic energy. Your Armor score is now increased by an amount equal to your INT score.
Prerequisite: Telekinesis
You have learned to use your control over kinetic forces to help keep you alive in times of crisis. Whenever you make a Fortitude or Reflex save that involves you moving an object or keeping something at bay, you can add your INT score instead of your STR or AGI score, as you use your Telekinesis to either help you move the item or alter its path so that you are no longer in its way.
You have learned to connect directly to the minds of those around you. You can now open a telepathic communication channel at will with a number of creatures up to your INT score within a number of feet equal to 10 times your Psychokinetic level. This channel can be one or two-way, and lasts until you dismiss it, also at will.
In addition, you can use an action to read the surface thoughts or emotions of one creature within range. When you do, they must make a Willpower save against your Psycho Save DR; on a success, they are aware of what you are trying to do and can choose to let you in or keep you out.
Lastly, you can now use your Energy Manipulation feature on attacks that deal psychic damage (this does not apply to Entropic Redirection).

Journeyman


Prerequisite: Telepathy
You have developed the power of your mind enough to scramble the minds of other creatures. Using this feat, pick a single creature that you can see within range of your Telepathy; that creature must make a Willpower save against your Psycho Save DR. On a failed save, a creature takes final psychic damage equivalent to 1d6 + your INT score and is stunned until the start of its next turn. On a successful save, they take half damage and are not stunned.
Additionally, you can now store and redirect psychic damage using your Entropic Redirection feature if you couldn’t already.
Prerequisite: Telepathy
Your ability to tap into the minds of others can be used to distract your enemies or enhance the focus of your allies. As an action, you can touch the mind of creature within range of your Telepathy and force them to make a Willpower save against you Psycho Save DR; if they fail, they have disadvantage on their next check, attack(s), or save for the next minute. In addition, if a creature within range of your Telepathy is forced to make a Willpower save, you can use your reaction to give them advantage or disadvantage.
Additionally, you can now store and redirect psychic damage using your Entropic Redirection feature if you couldn’t already.
This feat allows you to redirect any damage that you have stored into a physical weapon, allowing it to deal additional damage equal to the amount and type of damage that you stored. This damage applies only to the next attack made with that weapon.
Prerequisite: Psychic Armament
You are now able to redirect any energy you have stored into your conjured weapon, allowing it to deal an additional damage equal to the amount and type of damage that you stored. This damage applies only to the next attack made with that weapon.
Prerequisite: Telekinesis
You are now able to lift and control yourself using your Telekinesis, granting you a flying speed of 30 feet.
Prerequisite: Telekinesis
With this feat, you can use an action to create a barrier of pure telekinetic energy, warding off most things that would attempt to pass through it. The bounds of this barrier cannot exceed the range of your Telekinesis, and it lasts a number of minutes equal to double your INT score or until you dismiss it. This barrier is completely transparent, 1 foot thick, can be any shape that you choose, and can take up as much as 1000 square feet (so it can be a wall 20 feet high and 50 feet long, a sphere roughly 20 feet in diameter, or a cube with a face length of just over 10 feet, etc.). Creatures targeted by ranged attacks originating from the opposite side of the barrier have their RaDEF increased by an amount equal to your Psycho Save DR. A creature attempting to get through the barrier must succeed on a STR check against your Psycho Save DR to do so. You can use this feature a number of times between rests equal to your INT score.
Prerequisite: Clairvoyance
Psychometry is the ability to sense the last emotions or thoughts of the last creature to have touched an object; using this feat, you can use an action to sense the last emotional state and thought of the last person to have touched an object you are holding. Between rests, you can do this a number of times equal to your INT score.
Prerequisite: Telekinesis
Your Telekinesis has grown and developed to allow you to use an action (or two on especially large/dense objects) to reshape the objects you control, such as warp a sword or sharpen a blunt rock. To do this, you must already be controlling the object telekinetically. You cannot reshape an enchanted item unless it is already moldable.
Prerequisite: Telepathy
You’ve mastered your mind enough to protect it from any outside sources or influences. Your thoughts can no longer be read by any means unless you allow it and you no longer divide your chosen ability scores by 2 when determining your Willpower score.

Expert


You’ve become far more efficient in your Energy use, allowing you to make two Energy attacks before running out of whatever damage you’ve stored. Additionally, you can now store the damage for twice as long. If you have taken the Energized Weapon feat, then you may now energize two different weapons, allow one weapon to make two energized attacks, or give a single weapon one energized attack and keep the remaining energy for yourself. If you have taken the Energy Blade feat, then your conjured weapon can attack using your stored energy twice if you so desire.
Prerequisite: Telepathy
This feat allows you to use an action to probe deeper into a creature’s mind than just their surface thoughts and feelings. To do this, pick a single creature in range of your Telepathy; that creature must then make a Willpower save against your Psycho Save DR. On a success, they know what you are attempting to do and can choose to let you in or keep you out; on a failure, it knows you are probing its mind but cannot keep you out, and you know the reasoning behind its actions, its emotional state, and something important to or about it.

Master


Prerequisite: Telekinesis
You have become proficient enough at moving inanimate objects that you are now able to exert control over living creatures as well. Your ability to control living creatures follows the same restrictions as controlling objects; however, whenever you attempt to control a creature using your Telekinesis, it must make a Fortitude or Willpower save against your Psycho Save DR. On a failed save, it is controlled by you as if it were an inanimate object. If you attempt to directly harm the creature (such as tearing off limbs or crushing organs), it must make a Fortitude or Willpower save against your Psycho Save DR to resist what you are attempting to do to it. It can voluntarily fail these saves if it so chooses. Damaging non-vital organs or appendages deals 2d8 true damage.
Prerequisite: Telepathy
At 15th level, you have learned to fundamentally alter a creature’s worldview using the power of your mind, and in doing so effectively enslave it. Pick one creature that you can touch; that creature must make 2 Willpower saves against your Psycho Save DR as you use the next minute to attempt to enslave it; one save at the start of the process, and one at the end. During this time, you are both incapacitated, and if you take any damage or are otherwise violently disturbed, your trance ends and they automatically succeed on their second save. If they pass either of these saves, or if your trance is interrupted during this time, nothing happens, they are aware of what you tried to do, you cannot attempt to enthrall them again for another 15 days, and you cannot attempt to enthrall any other creature for 24 hours.
If they fail both of these saves, they become your thrall and are considered charmed by you. They now follow your every order to the letter, including attacking any creatures they were previously allied with, but not including deliberately hurting or killing themselves. You are always aware of your thrall’s distance and direction from you, can read their every thought, and can always communicate with them telepathically, all regardless of distance, so long as you are both on the same plane.
If you or one of your close allies attacks your thrall, and once every 48 hours after becoming enthralled, they can make another Willpower save, and on a success are no longer enthralled by you; if this happens, you are aware of it immediately, as well as the former thrall’s last known distance and direction from you if it was on the same plane as you when the effect ended. The effect also ends if you so choose. You can have only one thrall at a time; if you wish to gain a new thrall while you already have one, you must end the effect on that one. You must take a day’s rest before attempting to create a new thrall.
Prerequisite: Telepathy
This feat allows you to change up to 10 minutes of a creature’s memory. To do this, pick a single creature within range of your Telepathy, who must then make a Willpower save against your Psycho Save DR. On a success, they know what you are trying to do and can choose to let you modify their memory or not. On a failure, they are unaware of what you are doing, and you can select any 10 minutes of their memory and modify it as you see fit, inserting or removing aspects at your discretion. This change is permanent unless you later choose to un-modify it, which requires the same process. This can be performed once between day’s rests.
