Mutant

Unnatural Abilities

Mutants are Sentients who have undergone specific medical, alchemical, or arcane procedures – voluntarily or otherwise – that have left them with enhanced speed, strength, reflexes, and a bevy of other enhanced skills and abilities. Though they gain much, their abilities do come at a cost: Mutants age twice as quickly as normal, and can never again return to how they once were. Despite this, many Mutants welcome their new powers, or at the very least put them to very good use.

Class Features

As a Mutant you gain the following class features.

Health

Health at 1st Level: 10 + your CON score

Health Beyond 1st Level: 1d10 (or 5) + your CON score per Mutant level after 1st

Proficiencies

Skills at 1st Level:

  • You gain 2 WP to place in any Weapon Skill(s) of your choice;
  • You gain 1 proficiency in Alchemy Supplies, up to 5

Skills Beyond 1st Level: You gain 1 SP every time you level up and 1 WP at every even-numbered level.

Fearless

At 1st level, your mutated nature grants you greatly increased confidence and bravery, granting you advantage on all saves against being frightened.

Infrared Vision

The alterations to your body grant you the ability to see in the thermal spectrum, granting you infrared vision if you didn’t already have it.

Mutations

Lastly at 1st level, you gain what sets you apart from a normal Sentient: your Mutations, strange alterations to your body that grant you special abilities. You have 1 Mutation at this level, and gain more as you level up, as shown in the Mutant class table. Over the course of a night’s rest, you are able to change out one of your Mutations for another. Over the course of a day’s rest, you can change all of your Mutations if you so desire.

Class Feats

When you reach 2nd level, the changes in your body have matured enough that they have started to grant you more specialized abilities. At the end of the class description, under “Class Feats”, you may select one Novice class feat.

At 7th level, you may select either one Journeyman or two Novice class feats.

When you reach 13th level, you may choose either one Expert, one Journeyman and one Novice, or three Novice class feats.

Lastly, at 18th level your body is barely that of a Sentient anymore, and you may now select one Master, one Expert and one Novice, two Journeyman, one Journeyman and two Novice, or four Novice class feats.

If a feat has any prerequisites, you must meet those prerequisites before you can take it. Feats cannot be taken more than once unless they explicitly say so. Your feat choices are permanent and cannot be changed.

Enhanced Dodge

At 3rd level, your ability to anticipate and avoid incoming danger is unsurpassed. From now on, when determining your Dodge score, double your AGI and DEX scores, and for your Reflex score, do not divide them by 2.

Improved Physiology

Also at 3rd level, your abnormal body grants you advantage on saves against poison and disease, as well as resistance to poison damage.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one of your ability scores by 1, two of them by .5, or take a classless feat. You cannot increase an ability score beyond 5 via this feature.

Superior Reflexes

At 5th level, your ability to save yourself from incoming harm is extraordinary. From now on, whenever you are exposed to a damaging effect that targets an area and/or does not target you specifically, such as a trap, you can double your Dodge score when reducing damage from that effect.

Tireless

Your enhanced body tires far less easily, making you resistant to fatigue damage at 6th level.

Veins of Poison

At 9th level your blood and saliva become toxic to others. If a creature ingests your blood or is bitten by you, they take 2d6 + your CON score poison damage, and must make a Fortitude save against your CON check or become sickened for the next minute. Except for their next turn, they can repeat this save at the end of each of their turns to end the effect early.

Backup Mutagen

You have learned how to keep a single Mutation in reserve just in case. Starting at 14th level, once between rests, you can take one minute to swap out one of your Mutations for another. This backup Mutation must be decided at the end of your last rest, and over the course of a night’s rest you can change your backup Mutation as well as one of your active Mutations.

Final Form

You are a Mutant unlike any other, more powerful and capable than nearly all of your kind. At 20th level you are able to keep 2 backup Mutations instead of 1, and changing from one of your active Mutations to one of your reserve ones now requires only 2 actions instead of a full minute. Over the course of a night’s rest, you can alter both of your backup Mutations in addition to one of your active ones.

Mutant Class Feats

The reality of a Mutant’s very existence is that they are unnatural, they are freaks who have been twisted by experimentation into something beyond any Sentient. But this does not have to be a bad thing: many Mutants put their enhanced physique and skills to good use. The Mutant class feats help Mutants to achieve this, and make them more powerful than they already are.

Novice


Prerequisite: Bloodlust
You can now enter your Bloodlust state 4 times between rests. Additionally, the Willpower save DR increases to 11.
Twice between rests, whenever you deal final damage to an enemy, you can choose to enter a state of bloodlust for the next minute. While in this state, you gain resistance to physical damage and can choose to grant yourself advantage on as many attacks as you wish; however, if you grant yourself advantage on > 1 attack, all attacks against you also gain advantage until the start of your next turn. Lastly, at the start of each of your turns, you must succeed on a DR 10 Willpower save or attack a random creature within 10 feet of you; if there isn’t a creature within 10 feet of you, you attack the closest creature or object.
This feat allows you to improve one of your active Mutations, and how each Mutation is bettered is given here:
Animal Senses: Increases your Perceive proficiency by 3, to a maximum of 6;
Camouflage: Takes only an action to use;
Danger Sense: Increases your Perceive proficiency by 3, to a maximum of 6;
Enhanced Physique: Increases your chosen ability score by 2 instead of 1;
Fangs and Claws: Deals 1d8 base damage;
Fins: Increases your Swim proficiency by 3, to a maximum of 6;
Gills: Allows you to breathe both underwater and in dangerous gases;
Quadruped: Increases your crawling speed to 15 feet beyond your normal movement;
Rabidity: Grants you 2 extra attacks instead of 1;
Regeneration: Restores 2 Health at the start of each of your turns;
Scales: Increases your Armor by 4 instead of 2;
Spider Hair: Grants you a climbing speed equal to double your movement;
Spider Silk: Extends the range of your silk up to 60 feet and allows you to pull objects that are being worn or carried (requiring contesting STR checks);
Tail: Becomes twice as long;
Temperature Acclimation: Cold: You become immune to freeze damage;
Temperature Acclimation: Heat: You become immune to burn damage;
Tentacles: Increases the range of your arms to 30 feet;
Wings: Increases your flying speed to 50 feet.
You gain 10 extra feet of movement and 1 proficiency in both Climb and Swim, up to 5.
Your bulkiness grants you extra protection, causing your Armor score to increase by an amount equal to your STR score.
Your initiative bonus is now your AGI score + your DEX score.

Journeyman


Prerequisite: Bloodfeast
You can now enter your Bloodlust state 8 times between rests. Additionally, the Willpower save DR increases to 12.
Prerequisite: Boosted Mutation
You can now improve 2 of your active Mutations instead of 1.
Your unnatural speed and reflexes allow you to act quicker in combat, granting you an additional action.
Prerequisite: Nimble
Your mutated speed allows you to always be ready and act quickly in combat. From now on, whenever you roll initiative, treat a roll of 9 or lower as a 10 before adding your initiative bonus.
You gain the ability to tell lies from truth. You can automatically discern the presence of illusions when within 10 feet of them and you aren’t blinded or deafened; additionally, if a shapechanger not in their true form becomes the focus of your attention, is within 30 feet of you, and you are not blinded or deafened, you immediately become aware of the fact that they are a shapechanger, but not what their true form is.

Expert


You’re too slippery to be hit. From now on, whenever you take the Evade action, attacks against you deal minimum damage.
Prerequisite: Enhanced Mutations
You can now improve 3 of your active Mutations instead of 2.

Master


Prerequisite: Superior Mutations
You are now able to improve all 4 of your active Mutations to their better forms.
Forsaking your Sentient body almost entirely, this feat allows you to take a 5th Mutation.

Mutations


Your Perceive proficiency increases by 2, to a maximum of 5.
Over the course of the next minute, you are able to change the color of your skin to match your immediate environment. This causes all Stealth checks made by you to have advantage until you move. Depending on your environment, you may continue to have advantage until either your environment or your skin changes again.
Your Perceive proficiency increases by 1, up to 5, and you cannot be surprised.
Pick your STR, AGI, DEX, or CON score; that score increases by 1, up to 6.
You grow a set of savage and razor-sharp fangs and claws. These weapons deal 1d6 physical base damage and scale with the Unarmed skill.
Your fingers and toes become webbed, increasing your Swim proficiency by 2, up to 5.
You develop a set of gills that allow you to breathe underwater.
You are able to run just as well on all 4s as on your feet, making your crawling speed equal to your normal movement speed.
You gain 1 extra attack every time you take the Attack action.
You regain 1 Health at the start of each of your turns while you are not Enduring.
You grow a set of scales all over your body that increase your Armor score by 2. The aesthetics (color, pattern(s), etc.) of these scales are entirely up to you at the time you take this mutation.
You grow thin, complex, microscopic hairs on your feet and hands, granting you a climbing speed equal to your walking speed and allowing you to climb upside-down and on vertical surfaces. These hairs also stick to flesh, allowing you to rip a creature’s skin off; they count as Unarmed weapons for you, and deal 1d3 physical base damage.
You become able to use an action to shoot a length of sticky silk out of both of your wrists, out to a length of 30 feet. You can use another action to ascend or descend the silk’s length, swing from one location to another, or pull objects to you, so long as they weigh less than you and are not being worn or carried.
Out of the base of your spine, you grow a prehensile tail that is long, strong, and flexible enough to essentialy function as a 3rd arm.
You become resistant to freeze damage.
You become resistant to burn damage.
Your arms gain the ability to stretch far beyond their normal limits, allowing you to extend them up to 15 feet.
You grow a set of wings out of your back that grant you a flying speed of 30 feet.
