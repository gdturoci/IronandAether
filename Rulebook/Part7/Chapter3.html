Chapter 3: Concoctions and Enchants

Concoctions

Given here are all of the concoctions within I&A, along with the type of Aether required to brew it and the typical cost to buy it; however, it is up to your GM to decide how many vials of a given solution a seller has, if any, and whether or not it can be bought anywhere else. Unless a concoction’s description says otherwise, it must be drunk or injected in its entirety for its effects to be felt. A single person can be under the influence of up to 3 concoctions at once, including concoctions with instantaneous durations; if a 4th one is imbibed, the drinker is sickened for the next minute, at the end of which they immediately spend 2 actions throwing up and take 1d6 true damage. If a concoction requires two or more types of Aether, it needs at least 1 pound of each type to be brewed. To identify a concoction and its effects requires a successful DR 16 Alchemy Supplies or Arcana check, though the GM may wish to modify this DR based on the strength and nature of the brew.

To determine the cost of a concoction, use the following steps as a guide (if an NPC is selling the concoction, use the prices given):

  1. Double the cost of the Aether used to brew it;
  2. Double the cost of the other resources, or double the number of days to gather resources;
  3. Round that up to the nearest multiple of 10.

This should present a fair price for the concoction, though there may be other factors that modify the price of the brew at the seller’s discussion.

For simplicity’s sake, the Fortitude Save DR for each strength of poison is given here: Weak: 10; moderate: 20; strong: 30; exalted: 42. The described effects assume the imbiber failed the save; if they succeeded, the poison has no effect, unless they deal poison damage, wherein like in all other instances, this damage is reduced by the result of the victim's Fortitude save.

Enchants

Enchants are powerful and usually very helpful items – unless the item in question is cursed, though this is rarely the case (for a list of example curses, consult “Relic Curses” here). An enchant’s description will say its effects, power level, the Aether used to make it, and how it changes as its power level increases, if it can.

All enchants must be attuned to in order for the user to gain their benefits and know their capabilities. To attune to an item, the user must spend a number of hours equal to the enchant’s power level squared focusing on and bonding with the item in question during a rest; the exception to this is artifacts, which take a full day's rest to become attuned to. A person can be attuned to up to 3 enchants at a time. Unattuning to an enchant takes only an hour, and only one person can be attuned to an enchant at a time.

Because the amount of possible enchanted items is practically infinite, it would be impossible and impractical to attempt to list them all; instead, a few items will be given at each power level for each category of enchant to give an idea of what each type of item at each power level is like.

Determining the cost of an enchant is far easier than deciding the cost of a concoction; because so much Aether is used to create them, simply doubling the cost of the amount of Aether used to enchant the item provides a decent base to start with, and then the cost can be modified by the power level of the item at the seller’s discretion. Refer to the table below as a guide on modifying an enchant’s cost by power level.

Enchant Cost by Power Level
Power Level Cost Modification
1st Level + 1d4 x 10 Aeons
2nd Level + 2d6 x 12 Aeons
3rd Level + 3d8 x 15 Aeons
4th Level + 4d10 x 20 Aeons
Artifact + 5d20 x 25 Aeons

Destroying an Enchant

Enchants are harder to destroy than regular items, as befits their arcane nature. To destroy an enchant, one has two options: the first, which will only work on enchants of 1st and 2nd level, is to simply assail the item with overwhelming force and power, such as tossing it in an active volcano, smashing it repeatedly with a hammer or similar implement, or assault it with a variety of powerful and destructive spells. The second is to first disenchant the item, then destroy it as one would normally; the process for this is covered in the previous chapter.

4th Level

Aether. Life, 4th level and stronger; Earth and Water, artifact.
Description. A phylactery can be any container that fits within < 1 cubic foot of space, and allows one to store a bit of their life essence – a piece of their soul – inside it, ensuring their continued existence in the event their current body dies. A phylactery will not supply a new body for the deceased, but simply stores their soul for an indefinite time until a new unoccupied body can be supplied (or the phylactery is destroyed), such as a corpse, Homunculus, or Automaton that has yet to Awaken. An Undead being, such as a Skeleton or Zombie, does not qualify. In the event a corpse is to be used, it must have died of a cause other than old age and have no missing vital organs, such as its heart, lungs, or brain; if both of these qualifications have been met, then the power of the phylactery will restore the corpse to 1 Health, healing any mortal wounds. The unoccupied body must be within 10 feet of the phylactery to be possessed.
Higher Levels. As an artifact, a phylactery can create a… serviceable new body for the user. This new body forms over the course of 1d12 days, and is clearly not a normal Sentient body. In short, it has the appearance of being a half-rotted corpse, and while it will have all the necessary appendages (two arms and legs, two eyes, etc.), it will also have several chunks of flesh missing, such as exposed bones and sinew, a missing nose, etc. The power of the phylactery keeps the body alive, but only for so long; over the course of 1d12 months, the new body decays beyond the point of usability. The user can choose between a body constructed by the phylactery or possessing an unoccupied body.
Destroying a phylactery. A phylactery does not follow the ordinary rules for being destroyed. Essentially, a phylactery is the only 4th level/artifact enchant that can be destroyed without needing to be disenchanted, and can be destroyed as though it were a 1st/2nd level object. This is because the power of the phylactery is so extremely funneled into retrieving and containing the attuned soul – and providing it with a new body – that it leaves relatively very little power to protect the container itself.

Description. This shield grants special protection against Aetherians and spell-casting enchants. This shield grants resistance to any damage dealt by spells, and advantage on any save against spells and their effects. Weapons

Most every enchanted weapon can actually take the form of any weapon regardless of the name, e.g. a Biting Blade does not need to be a blade but can be any weapon. This does not mean that the weapon changes shape on command, just that when the GM decides to place a weapon in their world, it can be any kind of weapon that they desire.


1st Level

Aether. Light.
Description. This weapon sheds bright light in a 20 foot radius and dim light for another 20 feet at the user’s discretion.
Higher Levels. At 2nd level, it sheds 40 feet of bright and dim light; at 3rd level it sheds 80 feet of bright and dim light; at 4th level, 120 feet of each; and as an artifact, it sheds 240 feet of bright and dim light.
2nd Level

Aether. Air, Earth, Space, or Time.
Description. This weapon increases the user’s proficiency in the same type as the weapon itself by 1, up to 6.
Higher Levels. At 3rd level, proficiency increases by 2, up to 6; at 4th level, it increases proficiency by 3, up to 6, and grants a +1 bonus to attacks; as an artifact, it increases the user’s proficiency by 4, up to 6, and grants a +2 bonus to attacks.

Description. This weapon has 4 charges and can expend 1 charge to teleport the user to any unoccupied space they can see within 15 feet using an action. It regains 1d3 charges at midnight.
Higher Levels. At 3rd level, the number of charges increases to 6, the range increases to 30 feet, and the number of charges regained becomes 1d4+1. At 4th level, the number of charges increases to 8, the range of the teleport increases to 60 feet, and the number of charges regained becomes 1d6+2. As an artifact, it has 12 charges, the range is 120 feet, and it regains 1d8+4 charges at midnight.
