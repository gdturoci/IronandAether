Chapter 4: Relics

Relics are enchanted items that are so incredibly, wondrously powerful, and the process to create them so complex and beyond Sentient understanding, that their kind cannot be replicated; in short, Sentients cannot recreate the power of relics, which may be for the best, as many of them hold incredible destructive potential within them. So great is their power that simply by attuning to them – which, strangely enough, takes only an hour – the user will be both blessed with some sort of boon and cursed in some way.

You can attune to only one relic at a time and cannot unattune from it without destroying the relic. The features, including boons and curses, bestowed by a relic you have attuned to last until the relic is destroyed or you die; some relic benefits require the relic to be on your person, and a relic’s description will say if this is the case. If you die and are resurrected within 24 hours, such as through a Phylactery, the relic is still attuned to you; however, if you die and are not resurrected within that time, the relic is no longer attuned to you. During this 24 hours another person cannot attune to the relic. Lastly, if you ever lose or misplace the relic, it reappears in your possession next midnight.

Given here is a list of relics, along with the boons and curses gained from attuning to them, and some lore to give them some backstory and an idea of their power, though the information given is vague enough to allow the GM to modify it at their leisure. Any and all of the information pertaining to a relic, from their very existence to their powers, may be known or unknown to the characters, or even to the common populace, depending on the nature and history of your GM’s world and their discretion. You may learn this knowledge through research, from an ally, or through attuning to the relic itself.

In addition to its normal properties, which are gained when the user attunes to the relic, each relic can also be awakened, which occurs after the user has unlocked the relic’s true inner potential. The requirements to do this vary from relic to relic, and a relic’s description will say what it needs in order to awaken.

Lastly, destroying a relic is, as one might imagine, not easy. Each relic has its own special requirements to be destroyed, detailed in the relic’s description; otherwise, the relic is immune to damage.

Relics

Lore. Throughout the pages of history this mythical sword has gone by many names: Excalibur, Masamune, Tyrfing, Andúril. Throughout all of the legends, one commonality arises: a blade of great and unmatched power, that makes kings and legends of its wielders. It is said that, throughout its life, Excalibur has slain millions, be it through warfare, single combat, public execution, or any other method. Today, no Sentient can possibly know where history ends and legends begin, but any who wield this mythical blade quickly find that with a weapon like this at your side, such feats are not outside the realm of possibility.

Boons and Curses. Excalibur bestows the following boons and curses: 2 Minor Boons, 1 Major Boon, 1 Minor Curse, and 1 Major Curse. Upon awakening, it bestows 1 more Major Boon and 1 more Minor Curse.

Properties. When attuned to Excalibur, the user’s STR or DEX (their choice) score immediately increases by .5, to a maximum of 6. Additionally, while Excalibur is always described as a sword, the user can use an action to transform it into any Small, Medium, or Large weapon. Whatever form the weapon takes, and regardless of the user’s proficiency with the appropriate skill, attacks with it are made at 5 proficiency and have a +5 bonus. At the start of combat, the user also gains a +1d6 bonus to their initiative roll. Additionally, the mythical blade grants a +3 bonus to the user’s Armor score so long as Excalibur is on their person.

Excalibur’s final feature is also its most noteworthy: at any time while the user is holding it, they can choose to instantly engulf the blade in ice, fire, lightning, acid, venom, or psychic energy. Whichever they choose, the sword deals one extra die of damage of the appropriate type: freeze for ice, burn for fire, electric for lightning, caustic for acid, poison for venom, or psychic for psychic energy. So, if Excalibur were to become a dagger engulfed in flame, attacks made with it would deal 5d4 physical + 1d4 burning damage.

Awakening. Should Excalibur be wielded by a leader – such as someone with royal blood or an elected ruler – its true power unlocks.

Awakened Properties. Once awakened, Excalibur’s power increases the user’s previously chosen ability score by another .5 (up to 6), and it can now change form instantly. Additionally, the user’s proficiency when wielding Excalibur increases to 6, as does the bonus to its attacks. The initiative bonus also increases to +1d12, and the +3 Armor bonus becomes +6. The last change is that the damage of the chosen engulfment increases to two extra damage dice.

Destruction. Somewhere in the world, legend tells of a lake from whence Excalibur was gifted to its original wielder, whose name and deeds have been lost to time. Many scholars and mythologists believe that, should Excalibur be returned to this lake, it would disappear from the world forever, never to be seen or used again. Throwing the sword into the lake requires a successful DR 30 Willpower save; should the user fail the save, they must wait 24 hours before they can try again.

Lore. The Handcannon is a perfect marriage of firepower and arcana. What appears as a single large revolver is, in fact, much more.

Boons and Curses. The Handcannon bestows the following boons and curses: 1 Minor Boon, 1 Major Boon, and 2 Minor Curses. Upon awakening, it bestows 1 more Minor Boon.

Properties. For the most part, the Handcannon functions as a normal revolver, except for a few notable differences: firstly, upon attuning, the user’s Firearm proficiency increases by 1, up to 6; secondly, its base damage is 1d8; and thirdly, the user gains a bonus to Handcannon attacks equal to their Firearm proficiency.

The final and much more noteworthy difference between the Handcannon and an ordinary revolver is the Arcane Shot, which causes a random arcane effect to occur every time the Handcannon is fired. Roll on the following table to determine which effect occurs whenever you fire the Handcannon.

d6 Arcane Effect
1 Your bullet deals an additional 1d6 burn damage and sets Tiny or smaller items on fire.
2 Your bullet deals an additional 1d6 freeze damage and freezes Tiny or smaller items. It also freezes up to 2 cubic feet of water, even if that water is part of a larger body.
3 Your bullet deals an additional 1d6 poison damage and your target must make a Fortitude save against the amount of final damage dealt or be sickened for 1 minute.
4 Your bullet deals an additional 1d6 physical damage and if your target is Big or smaller, they must make a Fortitude save against the amount of final damage dealt or be pushed back 5 feet. If they are Tiny, they are pushed back 10 feet. Unenchanted and unworn items are also pushed back and have a hole blown into them that is about 3-6 inches in diameter and 1-3 inches deep, depending on the material.
5 Your bullet deals an additional 1d6 electric damage and electrifies water in a 2-foot radius for an instant, centered on where the bullet hit the water. Enemies hit by this effect cannot take reactions until the start of their next turn.
6 Your bullet deals an additional 1d6 caustic damage and corrodes metal in a 2-inch radius, centered where the bullet hit the metal. If your target is wearing unenchanted metal armor, the Armor bonus from that armor is reduced by 1.

Awakening. When the user fires the Handcannon at least once every day for one year, it will awaken. As it gets closer to awakening, it relays that to the user through some kind of stimulus, such as the appearance of decorative arcane runes or a growing sense of power and strength.

Awakened Properties. When it awakens, the Handcannon increases the user’s Firearm proficiency by another 1, up to 6, and the user can now choose which Arcane Effect occurs whenever you fire, instead of having to roll for it.

Destruction. To destroy a Handcannon, it must be subjected to each and every one of the Arcane Effects from another Handcannon by the next midnight. This means that it must be shot by one or more other Handcannons at least 6 times, until it has suffered each of the Arcane Effects, at which point it shatters. Alternatively, striking the Handcannon with each of Excalibur’s engulfments will also shatter it.

Lore. The Magnum Opus, Vitriol, the White Stone; the Philosopher’s Stone goes by all of these names and many more. Considered the absolute pinnacle of alchemy, the Stone makes kings and queens of ordinary folk and turns already powerful Mattemancers into near-demigods. It is unknown when or how the Stone was forged, and all attempts to replicate it have failed, but many historians and archaeologists have found material that suggests doing so requires the sacrifice of living beings.

Boons and Curses. The Philosopher’s Stone bestows the following boons and curses: 2 Minor Boons, 1 Major Boon, 1 Minor Curse, 1 Major Curse. Upon awakening, it bestows 1 more Minor Curse.

Properties. One of the most well-known features of the Philosopher’s Stone is that it can permanently turn any metal into an equal volume of another metal at the user’s discretion (e.g. one cubic foot of iron or lead can become one cubic foot of silver, gold, steel, bronze, etc.); similarly, it can transform any precious gem into any other precious gem, such as turning a ruby into a diamond, sapphire, or emerald.

Upon attuning to the Stone, the user’s proficiency with Alchemy Tools immediately increases by 3, up to 6. While the Stone is in their immediate possession, they can brew any concoction without the need for non-Aether resources and in half the time; likewise, enchanting items takes half as long as it normally would.

Additionally, the Stone has a pool of 10 Mana with which the user can cast any Mattemancer spells costing < 10 Mana; it restores this Mana at a rate of 1/hour. If the user is a Mattemancer, they instead have 5 Mana added to their maximum Mana while attuned to the Stone.

The third property of the Stone is that through it, the user can transmute their own flesh to heal their wounds and extend their life, something no Mattemancer is able to do unaided. While in possession of the Stone, the user can use an action and expend 1 Mana to restore 1d10 Health to any creature they can touch; for every 1 extra Mana they spend to do this, the amount of Health they restore increases by 1d10. Additionally, the Stone slows the user’s aging process; while attuned to it, the user ages 1 year for every 10 years that pass.

Fourth and lastly, the knowledge the Stone imparts increases the user’s INT score by .5, to a maximum of 6, and increases their Arcana and Nature proficiencies by 1, up to 6.

Awakening. Awakening the Philosopher’s Stone requires a similar process to the one that made it: to awaken the Stone, it must consume 100 liters of Mattemancer or Space Elementia blood (roughly 20 adult Humans). The Stone consumes blood as soon as it comes into contact with it, at a rate of half a liter per second.

Awakened Properties. When the Stone awakens, the Mana at its disposal doubles to 20, as does its repletion rate to 2 per hour; if the user is a Mattemancer, their maximum Mana increases by another 5. It also further increases the user’s INT score by another .5, up to 6, and increases their Arcana and Nature proficiencies by another 1, up to 6.

Destruction. Should the Stone be consumed by a pregnant Sentient, that child will be born a 2nd level Mattemancer and Space Elementia, with an additional amount of Mana at their disposal equal to half their Aetherian level (rounded up); additionally, this child will live 10 times as long as they otherwise would. When this child dies, the Philosopher’s Stone is destroyed. If the pregnancy does not come to term, such as through an abortion, miscarriage, or stillbirth, then the Sentient who ingested the Stone will vomit it back up when the fetus dies.

Lore. Beings with power second only to the gods, Titans are creatures of immense power and colossal size, of which only a handful have ever existed, and even fewer have been killed; to say that this is an incredibly difficult feat would be a massive understatement, but it is not impossible. However, when they are killed, that then allows the slayer to take one or more of their scales as a trophy, which themselves contain a fragment of the Titan’s power. Over time, a Titan’s body rots away and decays, and only two remnants of their existence remain: their skeletons, which are monumental in and of themselves; and fragments of their scales, which are occasionally found and dug up, granting their wielder the powers of a Titan.

Boons and Curses. A Titan Scale bestows the following boons and curses: 1 Minor Boon, 1 Major Boon, 1 Minor Curses, and 1 Major Curse. Upon awakening, it bestows 1 more Minor Boon and 1 more Minor Curse.

Properties. When the user attunes to a Scale, it becomes part of their body as it embeds itself in their flesh. In so doing, it imparts several of the Titan’s powers to the user, and for all of them except the STR increase and Drake fluency, the Scale has 3 charges at its disposal to use them; it regains all of the expended charges at midnight.

The first and second of these powers immediately increases the user’s STR score to 5 (or increases it by .5, up to 6, if the user’s STR score is already 5 or 5.5) and makes them fluent in Drake if they weren’t already.

The third and more noteworthy power is the Titan’s breath weapon. There are 6 possible breath weapons: acid, fire, ice, lightning, psychic energy, and poison; the obtainer of the Scale gains use of one of these (GM’s choice). At the cost of an action and 1 charge, the user can choose to exhale a stream of their breath weapon in either a 60-foot line or a 30-foot cone. In either case, every creature caught in the blast takes 6d6 of the appropriate damage (caustic for acid, burn for fire, freeze for ice, electric for lightning, psychic for psychic energy, and poison for poison).

The fourth and final power is the Titan’s flight, which manifests as a pair of magnificent wings that sprout from the user’s back at the cost of an action and 1 charge. These wings grant the user a flying speed of 45 feet for 1 hour.

The last property of the Scale is not a power of the Titan’s, but more its mark. When the user attunes to the Scale, in several random places on their body, scales of the Titan’s color begin to grow (black, blue, green, purple, red, or white), increasing the user’s Armor score by 2.

Awakening. Should the user obtain and “attune” to a second Scale from the same Titan, the Scale will awaken.

Awakened Properties. When the Scale awakens, the user’s STR score becomes 6 if it wasn’t already, and the Scale gains 3 more charges with which to use its powers. Additionally, each of the powers undergoes the following changes: the breath weapon’s range increases to 90 feet (line)/45 feet (cone), and the damage increases to 8d8; the flight speed increases to 60 feet; the scales grow, becoming thicker and covering more of your body, increasing your Armor score by another 2; and lastly, your Intimidation proficiency increases by 1, up to 6, as the might and sheer power of the Titan fills your body, mind, and soul.

Destruction. A Scale can only be destroyed by another Titan’s breath. A Scale has 50 Health (100 if awakened) separate from the user, and should the user be hit by another Titan’s breath weapon, any damage they take is likewise applied to the Scale. If the Scale’s Health is reduced to 0, it shatters, and the scales that have grown on the user’s body rot and fall off.
